Chapter 4 · Phonetic Inventory

The segmental sound system of Kelanese.

4.1 Overview

Kelanese exhibits a moderately sized consonant inventory with a preference for coronal and palatal articulations. Vowel contrasts are primarily qualitative rather than length-based.

This chapter describes *phonetic* realizations. Phonological patterning is treated in Chapter 5.

4.2 Consonants

4.2.1 Plosives

Plosives contrast primarily by place of articulation. Voicing is not phonemically contrastive.

maixaktel /ˌmaj.ʃakˈtel/
hesitate.PFV
“to hesitate”
While voicing is predictable, it may surface under fast or casual speech.

Regional Variation

Mainland varieties frequently palatalize /t/ before /i/.
